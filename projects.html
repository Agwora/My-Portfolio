Baby Sleep Prediction — Stochastic Process Model

Can we predict when a crying baby will fall asleep? A mathematical answer.

I started with a behavioral observation — a baby crying in cycles that gradually settle into sleep. I formalized this as a stochastic process with exponential decay for cry duration and logistic growth for pauses, incorporating autocorrelation, circadian modulation, and a random stopping time for sleep onset.

I implemented the model in Python as a generative simulation, then used Monte Carlo methods to estimate the distribution of sleep time given a start time of 21:00. The model produces a mean sleep time around 21:47 with a 95% prediction interval of about 30 minutes.

Key limitations: truncated normality for noise, no external interventions. This model should never replace parental attention. Future work: Bayesian hierarchical modeling across infants. The Rivalry Effect

Quantifying how conflict drives music video views — and what it actually means for long-term growth.

Two East African music videos. Same lead artist (Bien). Dramatically different trajectories. "Finale" hit 10M views in 11 days while "Katam" took 9 months. I decomposed view velocity, classified comment sentiment using basic NLP, and ran a two-proportion Z-test to isolate whether the spike was organic or conflict-driven. It was conflict-driven — and the data proved it.
